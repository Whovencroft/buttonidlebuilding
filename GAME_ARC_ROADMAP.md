# The Marble Arc: Game Progression Roadmap

This document outlines the overarching narrative and mechanical progression of the multi-game project. The core narrative thread is the "Marble"—an object that begins as a simple curiosity, evolves into a cosmic threat, becomes an object of petty revenge, and finally serves as the catalyst for an epic quest.

## 1. The Idle Game (Phase 1)
**Tone:** Curiosity, escalating complexity, eventual chaos.
**Mechanics:** Incremental clicker, resource management, unlocking modules.
**The Marble's Role:** The game begins with a simple button. Through upgrades and "containment failures," the button eventually morphs into the Marble.
**Transition:** The button/marble shatters its containment. The player follows the surviving marble piece through a secret tunnel, losing track of it. This transitions the player into the MUD.

## 2. The Marble Game (Phase 2)
**Tone:** Physics-based puzzle solving.
**Mechanics:** Navigating a marble through complex geometry.
**The Marble's Role:** The player directly controls the marble.
**Transition:** (Currently integrated into the Idle -> MUD transition, but exists as a distinct mechanical phase).

## 3. The MUD (Phase 3)
**Tone:** Exploration, mystery, genre-blending RPG.
**Mechanics:** Text-based multiplayer RPG, Circlemud-style combat, deep class/specialization system based on player personality quizzes, massive power scaling.
**The Marble's Role:** The marble is the ultimate objective. The player is always one step behind it, solving a massive meta-puzzle to find it.
**The Wizard Foreshadow:** One of the MUD's puzzle chains leads the player to a distraught wizard NPC. The wizard's domain shows clear evidence that the marble has passed through and caused havoc—destroyed wards, shattered artifacts, scorched research. The wizard is sympathetic, a victim. The player helps him or at least witnesses his desperation. This encounter plants the seed: the wizard has seen the marble's power firsthand and has reason to want it. When he later appears at the golf course to steal it, the player realizes this wasn't a random theft—it was a plan that started here.
**Transition:** At the end of the MUD, the marble absorbs immense power from an unknown source within the MUD world. It grows exponentially, shattering the boundaries of the text-based world and transitioning the game into a SHMUP.

## 4. The SHMUP (Phase 4)
**Tone:** High-octane action, cosmic threat.
**Mechanics:** Shoot 'em up (exact format TBD—either controlling a ship against waves with the marble as the boss, or a pure bullet-hell survival against the marble itself).
**The Marble's Role:** The marble is a giant, overpowered orb boss.
**Transition:** Upon defeating the giant marble, it shatters. Among the cosmic rubble, the player finds a small, perfectly round, intact chunk. Seeking revenge (and a break), the player takes this chunk to a golf course.

## 5. The Golf Game (Phase 5)
**Tone:** Comedic relief, petty revenge, relaxing but challenging.
**Mechanics:** 18-hole physics golf game, loosely inspired by *Kirby's Dream Course*.
**The Marble's Role:** The surviving chunk of the marble is literally used as the golf ball.
**Transition:** Upon completing the 18th hole, the Wizard from the MUD reappears—no longer distraught, but determined. He steals the marble/golf ball, declaring he now understands its power and intends to use it. He vanishes. The player recognizes him.

## 6. The Gold Box RPG (Phase 6)
**Tone:** Classic 90s tactical RPG, epic quest.
**Mechanics:** Top-down 2D, party-based (4-6 characters), grid-based tactical combat, stat-heavy exploration.
**The Marble's Role:** The stolen artifact that must be recovered from the Wizard to stop whatever scheme he has been building since his encounter with the marble in the MUD.
**Transition/Integration:** **Crucial Link:** The player's character from the MUD is ported directly into the Gold Box RPG. The MUD character's final stats, class structure, and power level serve as the starting point for the RPG protagonist. The player then hires allies to build out their party.

---

## Design Philosophy & Continuity
- **Mechanical Independence:** With the exception of the MUD -> Gold Box RPG character port, each game is mechanically independent.
- **Narrative Persistence:** The marble is the constant. Its state in one game directly causes the scenario of the next.
- **Thematic Shifts:** The project embraces wild genre shifts, using the transitions as narrative set-pieces to justify the changing mechanics.
